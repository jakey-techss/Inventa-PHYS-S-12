Inventa

Description

Inventa Kit & Studio is a combination set of software and hardware to help kids understand better how AI works and educate them beyonds just use of AI into curiosity concerning the systems behind AI. Using a simple drag and drop programming system and modules (which avoid depicting electrical concepts, keeping it simple) kids are able to use an Inventa kit to build robots that collect initial data, then use Inventa Studio (the web app) to train their robot and give it more functionality based off the data input.

The Concept

Components Of Inventa

Components Relationship To Inventa
  • Inventa makes use of Lego's assemble type construction
  • Inventa can be broken down and built up into other things ike legos
  • Makes use of Scratch's Drag-and-Drop Block System for the programming of robots
  • The whole goal of Inventa is for kids to feel comfortable building robots
  • This creates Inventa. Inventa is more than just robots but rather building smart bots that are able to capture and analyze data and respond based of the program written by the programmer

Why Inventa?

Inventa Kit & Studio connects with me a lot primarily because my love for computer science and coding came from Scratch and Legos. I want to be able to distribute that interest considering the current state of the technology world which is currently run by AI. Instead of kids and teens using AI Inventa hopes to foster a curiosity concerning AI creation hence inspiring the next generation of programmers, AI enthusiasts and robot builders.

ShotBot

86/100

ShotBot

Description

ShotBot is a funny, cool project that combines physics principles and robotics to make a robot that theoretically can never miss anywhere within its range of shooting. It is a fun toy that exemplifies the concepts of projectile motion and can be manually controlled with a web app. It could also be applicable when teaching projectile motion. The ShotBot will also be able to enter free-play mode whereby the user will be able to control the angle of inclination and force and make their own calculations.

The Concept

Components Of ShotBot

Components Relationship To ShotBot
  • ShotBot would be a robot capable of shooting, making noises and celebrating
  • No one shoots better than Steph Curry except ShotBot, the whole idea is to make a shooter that can launch a small ball into a destination given absolutely no measurements
  • It is impossible to talk about physics without talking about Isaac Newton himself, the concept of gravity drives the mechanism of ShotBot

R.G.F

Description

R.G.F (Resting GOAT Face) is the funniest of the list (mainly because of the name). It's essentially a robotic face that looks realistic in terms of its mouth moving when it is talking and it's life like voice. Adding facial expression would be great but highly unlikely in 7-weeks. The project essentially creates a face of one of the GOAT's of sports - Lionel Messi (Soccer), Stephen Curry (Basketball) or Max Verstappen (F1)
