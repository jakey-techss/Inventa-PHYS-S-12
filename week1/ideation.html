Project 01

Inventa Kit & Studio

91/100

Inventa

Description

Inventa Kit & Studio is a combination set of software and hardware to help kids understand better how AI works and educate them beyond just use of AI into curiosity concerning the systems behind AI. Using a simple drag-and-drop programming system and modules (which avoid depicting electrical concepts, keeping it simple), kids are able to use an Inventa kit to build robots that collect initial data, then use Inventa Studio (the web app) to train their robot and give it more functionality based off the data input.

The Concept

Components Of Inventa

Components Relationship To Inventa
  • Inventa makes use of Lego's assemble type construction.
  • Inventa can be broken down and built up into other things like Legos.
  • Makes use of Scratch's drag-and-drop block system for programming robots.
  • The whole goal of Inventa is for kids to feel comfortable building robots.
  • This creates Inventa. Inventa is more than just robots, but rather building smart bots that are able to capture and analyze data and respond based on the program written by the programmer.

Why Inventa?

Inventa Kit & Studio connects with me a lot primarily because my love for computer science and coding came from Scratch and Legos. I want to be able to distribute that interest considering the current state of the technology world which is currently run by AI. Instead of kids and teens using AI, Inventa hopes to foster a curiosity concerning AI creation, inspiring the next generation of programmers, AI enthusiasts and robot builders.

Project 02

ShotBot

86/100

ShotBot

Description

ShotBot is a funny, cool project that combines physics principles and robotics to make a robot that theoretically can never miss anywhere within its range of shooting. It is a fun toy that exemplifies the concepts of projectile motion and can be manually controlled with a web app. It could also be applicable when teaching projectile motion. The ShotBot will also be able to enter free-play mode whereby the user will be able to control the angle of inclination and force and make their own calculations.

The Concept

Components Of ShotBot

Components Relationship To ShotBot
  • ShotBot would be a robot capable of shooting, making noises and celebrating.
  • No one shoots better than Steph Curry except ShotBot. The whole idea is to make a shooter that can launch a small ball into a destination given absolutely no measurements.
  • It is impossible to talk about physics without talking about Isaac Newton himself. The concept of gravity drives the mechanism of ShotBot.

Why ShotBot?

Apart from the fact that it is an all-round cool project and it's a combination of many of my favorite things (Stephen Curry, robots and physics), it can also be a great tool to explain and experiment with Newton's Laws, particularly pertaining to projectile motion and an object undergoing acceleration strictly due to gravity, improving understanding of a core concept in physics and the real world seen in every ball sport.

Project 03

R.G.F

86/100

R.G.F

Description

R.G.F (Resting GOAT Face) is the funniest of the list (mainly because of the name). It's essentially a robotic face that looks realistic in terms of its mouth moving when it is talking and its life-like voice. Adding facial expression would be great but highly unlikely in 7 weeks. The project essentially creates a face of one of the GOATs of sports — Lionel Messi (soccer), Stephen Curry (basketball) or Max Verstappen (F1).

The Concept

Components Of R.G.F

Components Relationship To R.G.F
  • R.G.F's centerpiece rests at the construction of a robot with all the necessary parts to have a moving mouth and possibly facial expressions.
  • R.G.F is not complete without a GOAT's face and voice at the center of it. It may even be possible for it to speak different languages based on what is selected on the app.
  • The optimal solution for realistic voices for R.G.F, which is very vital for its ability to connect with people beyond its robotic nature.
